This simulation places participants in the role of a junior civil engineer working on an infrastructure project. It introduces core engineering responsibilities and encourages practical thinking around safety, materials, and construction planning. Designed for early-career learners, the tasks simulate a real-world engineering environment that emphasizes both technical analysis and written communication. In this simulation, participants will be tasked with evaluating a construction site, selecting appropriate materials, and drafting a project report.

Task 2 - Project Proposal

As part of your internship, you‚Äôve been asked to assist in drafting a concise project proposal for a pedestrian bridge over a small canal that links a residential area to a local park. The bridge will serve both practical commuting needs and recreational use. Your proposal will be reviewed by your engineering supervisor and potentially forwarded to local council stakeholders. Your goal is to summarize the key project elements in a format suitable for a non-technical audience, while still demonstrating basic engineering understanding and planning awareness.

Guidelines

  • Project Overview: Briefly describe the purpose and benefits of the bridge.
  • Design Considerations: Discuss materials, safety features, and environmental impact.
  • Site Considerations: Summarize key aspects such as soil type, water level changes, and accessibility for construction vehicles.
  • Budget Estimate: Provide a rough cost estimate for materials and labor.
  • Timeline: Outline a basic project timeline from design to completion.
  • Conclusion: Summarize the importance of the project and its expected impact on the community.

Task 3 - Site Plan Review

Following up with your project proposal, the engineering supervisor has some queries regarding the key risks of your project proposal. Analyse and explain the key risks in the project proposal and methods to mitigate these risks. (keep word count between 150-200 words for this task)
